## Closure-path summary

The 22 axioms decompose into four closure strategies:

### Mechanical byte-level (8 axioms)

The byte-layout axioms (`*_mu_input_*_spec`, `sign_layout_m_buffer_external_mu`)
and the structural-shape axioms (`combine_body_z_via_aggregation_spec`,
`combine_body_partial_responses_spec`) are pure byte-walk arguments. They
close by bridging extracted procedure regions to FIPS 204 byte-format
specifications. Estimated effort per axiom: days.

### FIPS 204 functional-op bridges (8 axioms)

The w-stage, z-stage sub-axioms and `pack_unpack_n1_signature_roundtrip`
reduce to bit-level pack/unpack identities on the abstract types. Closing
them means concretizing the abstract types against
`MLDSA65_Functional.pack_signature` / `MLDSA65_Functional.encode_sk` once
the FIPS 204 bit-level mechanization is in place. That mechanization is a
multi-month research project comparable to Barbosa–Barthe–Dupressoir's
Dilithium mechanization (CRYPTO 2023).

### Probabilistic Hoare (2 axioms)

The accepted-path no-reject axioms condition `status = 0` on the protocol-
level `accept_signing_attempt` event. Closing them requires a probabilistic
Hoare-logic chain that proves the kappa rejection-sampling loop terminates
on honest inputs. The accompanying `mldsa_accept_lower_bound` operational
bound — approximately $1 - 2^{-128}$ after the kappa-bounded loop — is
operational rather than mechanized.

### Lean bridge (5 axioms)

The five Lean-bridged algebraic axioms are mechanized on the Lean side and
connected by a CI-guarded citation bridge. **Replacing the bridges with
checked translation artifacts** is the right step toward 100% mechanized.
A translation layer that ingests both the Lean theorem statement and the
EC axiom statement and machine-verifies their semantic equivalence is its
own multi-week-to-multi-month project.

Alternatively, ported the Lean polynomial-Lagrange theory into EC itself
(estimated 3–4 weeks for a minimal interpolation theory sufficient to
discharge the five axioms).

## What this is not

The 22 axioms enumerate the **trust footprint** of the byte-equality
chain. They are not the only EasyCrypt declarations in the dependency cone;
the per-file accounting blocks distinguish axioms (trusted) from lemmas
(derived). The headline byte-equality theorem
`pulsar_n1_byte_equality_extracted` is itself a **proved lemma**, not an
axiom — its proof composes the wrapper-bridge equivs with the body-spec
lemmas, and trust reduces to the 22 axioms listed above.
