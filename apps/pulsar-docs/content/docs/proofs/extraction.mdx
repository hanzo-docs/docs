## What's a decomposition vs. what's a closure

Most of the version moves are **decompositions**: a single broader axiom
is replaced by narrower sub-axioms plus a proved structural composition.
Trust moves to smaller, independently-attackable obligations; total
axiom count typically stays the same or grows by 1.

A few moves are **real closures** where an axiom is removed without
replacement:

- **v11 codec** eliminated 3 codec axioms outright by concretizing
  `signature_t` as a record-wrapper structure
  (`encode_decode_signature`, `decode_encode_signature_wf` removed;
  `encode_signature_wf` narrowed to a length-only axiom;
  `encode_signature_len` derived via record-wrap structure).

## Accept-event conditioning (v13)

The v13 accept-bridge axiom `accept_signing_attempt_iff_R1234` connects
the bundled protocol-level `accept_signing_attempt` predicate to the four
per-rejection-condition predicates ($R_1, R_2, R_3, R_4$) used inside the
byte-walks. Each per-stage lemma after v10 is **conditioned on
`status = 0`** (the accepted-path precondition); the no-reject companion
axioms discharge the status bit from the protocol-level accept event.

This sidesteps the prior framing тАФ "the sign-side proof is harder because
of the kappa loop" тАФ by introducing `combine_body_compute_status` /
`sign_body_compute_status` and threading the accept event through each
per-stage lemma rather than over-the-loop.

## Real-closure summary

In addition to the 14 re-classifications, **3 codec axioms** were
**eliminated outright** in v11 (no replacement axiom):

- `encode_decode_signature` (removed)
- `decode_encode_signature_wf` (removed)
- `encode_signature_wf` (kept, narrowed to a length-only axiom)
- `encode_signature_len` тЖТ derived via record-wrap structure

## Recommended next proof work (post-submission)

Per the user's review prioritization (revised after v7):

1. `*_body_z_spec` тАФ likely easier than full `w_spec` with the Lean
   Lagrange bridge already stable. Use
   `Crypto.Threshold.Lagrange.threshold_partial_response_identity` for
   combine; reduce via vec ops + accepted-╬║ model for sign.
2. `*_body_mu_input_spec` тАФ byte-layout proof, mechanical and
   high-confidence once the FIPS 204 ┬з5.4.1 byte serialization is
   concretised.
3. `*_body_h_spec` тАФ bridge toward `vec_k_make_hint`.
4. `*_body_w_spec` тАФ the hardest target; requires the loop/fixed-point
   accepted-╬║ model + `ExpandA` / `ExpandMask` / `mat_vec_mul`.

This reverses the earlier ordering (which had `w` as the natural next
target after `w1`'s HighBits decomposition). The revised ordering
optimizes for fastest residual-trust reduction.
